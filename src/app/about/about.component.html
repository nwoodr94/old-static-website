About Me

I'm native to the Pacific Northwest, and I graduated from the University of Washington with a B.S. in Applied Physics in 2017. I performed an academic emphasis in mathemetics, but most of my research efforts involved the space sciences.

The day I graduated, I packed my car full of everything I owned and drove to Austin, TX.


My Programming Story

I first learned to code with a space science laboratory at the University of Washington, where I was challenged to design and build sounding rockets. I also engineered payloads, designed to explore the kinematics of flight, acquire state properties of the atmosphere, and transmit GPS telemetry.

To develop these payloads, I programmed an Arduino to communicate with a variety of commercially available sensors and instrumentation.

My first programming language was C.

I totally geeked out, even sometimes skipping class to work on my rocket. You can view my code here.

As an established developer, I intend to fund my own research into more advanced propulsion technologies, such as a hybrid configuration, and of course build on my experience with embedded systems.
