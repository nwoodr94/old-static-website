About Me


I'm native to the Pacific Northwest, and I graduated from the University of Washington with a B.S. in Applied Physics in 2017.

In the summer of 2019 I completed a full-stack software development bootcamp, and due to my interest in working with data, I started work as a software developer on a data team in Los Angeles.

My Programming Story


I first learned to code with a space science laboratory at the University of Washington, where I was challenged to design and build sounding rockets. These rockets carry payloads to explore the kinematics of flight, acquire state properties of the atmosphere, and transmit GPS telemetry.

To develop these payloads, I programmed an Arduino to communicate with commercially available sensors and instrumentation.

My first programming language was C.

I totally geeked out, even sometimes skipping class to work on my rocket. You can view the source code here.

Machine Preferences


I'm developing in Crostini on a Pixelbook Go, with the i5/8/128 gig configuration. I like the Chrome OS philosophy, and when it's time to code, I fire up the terminal and work directly in the provisioned Debian container. Linux is a lot of fun and the support is great.

While Google is my provider of choice for hardware and OS solutions, I stick with AWS for cloud-based development work.
