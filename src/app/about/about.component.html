About Me


I'm native to the Pacific Northwest, and I graduated from the University of Washington with a B.S. in Applied Physics in 2017.

In the summer of 2019, I completed a full-stack software development bootcamp, and began work in Los Angeles.

My Programming Story


I first learned to code with a space science laboratory at the University of Washington, where I was challenged to design and build sounding rockets. These rockets carry payloads to explore the kinematics of flight, acquire state properties of the atmosphere, and transmit GPS telemetry.

To develop these payloads, I programmed an Arduino to communicate with a variety of commercially available sensors and instrumentation.

My first programming language was C.

I totally geeked out, even sometimes skipping class to work on my rocket. You can view the source code here.
